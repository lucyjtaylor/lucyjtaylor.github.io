Award winning user-centred design training

I champion user-centred approaches to design wherever I work, and I?m a passionate advocate for the user. In fact, the Ministry of Defence even awarded me a Certificate of Outstanding Contribution and a commanders coin for introducing user-centred design (UCD) practices to their programme, improving outcomes across the board.

When I joined the MoD as a keen UX designer and user researcher, I was worried I would struggle to use my skills; my team were leading a Careers and Transformation Programme, but they rarely interacted with their users.

I made it my mission to coach colleagues in UCD practices, supporting them personally and through tailored training courses that I designed.

I took a few approaches:

I joined meetings for each project in the programme, and consistently advocated for user perspectives. When this caught colleagues’ interest, I offered recommendations of UCD practices and then helped them do that……….

I set up and tracked performance metrics for my own work. I showcased the data openly and regularly, to demonstrate where I was succeeding in making positive change. I supported colleagues to set up metrics for their own work, too

I designed and delivered a UCD training course for all team members. I provided practical advice on where to start wit UCD, simple and quick techniques to use when starting out, and links to supporting content for self-led learning so attendees could keep building confidence. I based examples on our existing projects and Army scenarios to make it really clear how and when these techniques could be used. Even the most senior Brigadier attended
